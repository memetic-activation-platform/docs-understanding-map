    MAP builds its approach to coordination on a deceptively simple idea: trust emerges from voluntary commitments. At the heart of this design is the Promise ‚Äî a sovereign, agentic declaration of intent.

    MAP extends Promise Theory in three key ways:

    3. Promises may carry Vital Capital ‚Äî knowledge, care, materials, social trust ‚Äî not just money or products.

    Systems of coordination in MAP are networks of promises, not chains of control.
